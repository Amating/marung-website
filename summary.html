What Is Marung?
A cloud-native financial access platform built for Africa's evolving digital economy.

Unlike traditional banks, Marung does not seek to hold deposits, lend money or operate as a licensed bank. Instead, Marung partners with regulated financial infrastructure providers to deliver digital wallet services, payments, financial access and future embedded financial solutions.

This asset-light approach allows Marung to remain agile, scale rapidly, adopt emerging technologies and focus on solving customer problems while leveraging trusted partners for regulatory compliance and payment infrastructure.

Marung owns the customer experience. Infrastructure partners provide the rails. Together, they create a more inclusive financial ecosystem.

The Problem
Money goes digital. Then it goes back to cash.

In 2025, FNB alone processed 69 million transactions worth R43 billion through its eWallet product. Every single one forced the recipient to withdraw cash — an ATM, a retailer queue, a PIN that expires in 16 hours. The digital journey ended every time, for every person.

This is not an FNB problem. It is the architecture of the entire South African informal payment system. Every major bank operates in its own silo. Millions of people receive money digitally and are immediately pushed back to cash.

The Story Behind Marung

In April 2025, our founder received money via FNB eWallet. It arrived digitally — on her phone, in seconds. And then the system did what it always does: it told her to go to an ATM to withdraw cash.

That moment became a question she could not stop asking. She spent two years researching the answer — studying SA payment regulations, completing cloud computing training, learning from domestic workers and SADC nationals about how they actually move money. She built the first working prototype herself — before any external funding, before any investors, before asking anyone for help.

Marung Sebaka Technologies was incorporated in March 2026. The name means a place in the clouds — accessible to everyone, from anywhere, on any phone.

The Market
A large, underserved market. A simple solution.
30 million+ South Africans are unbanked, underbanked, or paying per-transaction fees every time they move money. Marung's flat-fee model targets less than 1% of this addressable market to reach a sustainable business — a deliberately conservative entry point into a large and growing opportunity.

The comparable companies — Wave in West Africa ($1.7 billion), TymeBank in South Africa ($1.5 billion), Moniepoint in Nigeria ($1 billion) — all built on the same financial inclusion model. Marung is the South African equivalent with a SADC corridor strategy none of them have.
